# /resurrect

Revive useful genes or a whole genome from the graveyard. Optional argument names a buried timeline or gene pattern: $ARGUMENTS

1. Read `.claude/flux/graveyard/`. Find the buried timeline or gene pattern in question, or scan for patterns relevant to the current problem.
2. Check context. A gene flagged lethal in one context may be a survival trait in the current one. Compare the death context to the present problem context.
3. If the gene or genome fits the present context, compose it into a new timeline as `gen-001`, noting in the file that it was resurrected and from where, and why it should survive this time when it died before.
4. Announce the resurrection, naming the original cause of death and what changed about the context.

The ghost in the graveyard finds new life in a different world. This is FLUX's contextual resurrection: a toxic gene in one environment can be the right gene in another. The graveyard remembers not just what died, but where.
